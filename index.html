05Philosophy
Three people who ship something on Tuesday will always beat fifty who meet about it on Thursday.
  1. 01

    Purpose-built tools.

    We ignore hype cycles. We look for unglamorous, everyday problems — missed calls, cravings, macros, a chart nobody explained to you — and build software that solves them and stays out of the way.

  2. 02

    Speed to utility.

    No staging environments, no roadmap theatre. We ship the first working version in weeks, watch what people actually do with it, and let real use earn the next feature.

  3. 03

    Small on purpose.

    We could grow the team. We don't, because the same people who build RankLadder, Kaivo, and Ask Kazmo are the ones who read what customers say about them. Nobody here is more than one conversation away from the thing that's actually broken.

06How we work

Hunch to live, in a few weeks.

  1. Week 0

    Find the pain

    We look for unglamorous problems with a clear daily user and a willingness to pay. If we can't describe it in a sentence, we drop it.

  2. Week 1–3

    Ship v1

    A small team puts a working tool in front of real users inside three weeks. No staging, no waiting room.

  3. Month 2–6

    Find the loop

    We watch how people actually use it. The one behaviour that compounds gets all our attention. Everything else gets cut.

  4. Year 1+

    Keep it running

    We operate the products we build. Operators and capital come in only when growth says so, not before.
